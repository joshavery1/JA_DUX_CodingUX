Scannable & Snackable

When writing content it's important to consider the accessibility of the information. This can be done to improve the informations digestibility, and by this I'm referring to the terms 'Snackable' and 'Scannable'.

Before designing the content, the intention should be clear. Do you want the readers to scan this information and be able to understand the sentiment very quickly? Or, do you want the readers to be able to study the content to understand the detail in depth? The term 'snackable' refers to content that can be easily digested in smaller paragraphs. This can be particularly useful when writing product descriptions, for example, where the reader will want to understand the information fully. Alternatively, the term 'Scannable' refers to content where the reader can understand the general sentiment of the information very quickly by scan-reading the text, for example, an 'about us' section of a website.

There are studies that have found the ideal content design for achieving both snackability and scannability, with regards to line length, paragraph height and the visual hierarchy of the content.
